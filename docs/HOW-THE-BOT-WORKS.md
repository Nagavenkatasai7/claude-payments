# How the SmartRemit WhatsApp bot works

*Code-verified walkthrough (main @ 69a3130, 2026-06-12). Every claim below was traced to a file and line. Companion diagram: `diagrams/end-to-end-transfer-flow.*`*

## The story in one paragraph

A customer texts the WhatsApp number; Meta delivers it to our webhook, which checks Meta's cryptographic signature, drops duplicates, and writes the chat turn as a durable job in the Postgres outbox. A background worker picks the job up (instantly via a "poke", guaranteed every 5 minutes by GitHub Actions), loads the conversation from Redis, builds a partner-branded prompt with server-injected facts the model cannot fake, and calls the AI model (Kimi K2.6 on Ollama Cloud). The model can use 18 tools, but every dangerous one re-checks the rules server-side: identity verification before any send, dollar caps, sanctions screening that cannot be switched off. The money moment is the **Approve & Pay card**: the tool locks a real quote, saves a single-use 30-minute draft in Redis, and sends a card whose button is just a link to the hosted pay page. **The agent's job ends there — it never moves money.** Money moves only on the pay page: one-time code, bank details (never typed in chat), then a crash-safe mint that honors the exact quoted figures, the (mock-today) funding capture, and a single database transaction that flips the transfer to paid and queues the signed payout instruction to the partner's rail.

## Non-obvious behaviors worth knowing

- **At-least-once turns**: the dedupe key prevents double *enqueue*, not double *execution*. If the send to Meta fails after the LLM ran, the whole turn retries — money paths stay safe (single-use drafts, claim-first mints, rank-guarded status flips), but a duplicate chat reply is possible.
- **Media gets no reply at all** — photos/audio are silently dropped (parse returns null, webhook acks 200).
- **The Ollama call has no timeout and no fallback model** — hard-down AI means every turn answers "having trouble, send that again" until it recovers.
- **The model is never trusted with URLs or draft ids** — links are stripped and re-minted by code; the approve button's draft id is supplied server-side from the tap.
- **Delivered-status callbacks from Meta are logged only** — there is no wamid→transfer mapping yet, so a failed WhatsApp delivery of the "delivered" notice is invisible.
- **Real-rail delivered notices are fire-and-forget** (sent in `after()` in the payment webhook, not outbox rows) — the one known gap in the "every external effect is an outbox row" durability spine.
- **Attempts increment at claim time**, so a worker crash mid-turn progresses the row toward dead-letter instead of looping forever.
